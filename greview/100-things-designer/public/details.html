This visualization shows books often bought with the 100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People, written by Susan Weinschenk.

Key Observations

The book has a simple structure - every section is very short and insightful. You can quickly finish couple section when you have a few minutes to spare. At the same time the knowledge that you get is comparable to the usability classics: The Design of Everyday Things and Don't Make Me Think. No wonder they ended up in the same neighborhod on this procedurally generated map.

Presense of Startup cluster on the North West side of the island is curious to me. Maybe it is because succesful startup requires better than average user experience?

Personal Opinion

I finished reading 100 Things Every Designer Needs To Know About People in March of 2017. It is hard for me to point out a single thing that I'm applying daily in my life, but I'm sure these things have cummulative, hidden effect on everything I do.

If you are working on the fron-end side of the programming industry, please do your users and yourself a gift and read this book.
