Personal Opinion

I think Designing Data-Intensive Applications is a fundamental book, that should be in the library of every software engineer. In my mental bookshelf its place is next to the industry's classics (Design Patterns, Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture, and Clean Code).

The book's narrative is multidimensional, engaging, and complete. It touches past, present and future of the data management systems. At one moment you are diving deep into data storage formats, and at the other you are soaring high in the world of distributed data.

I am a very slow reader, and I spent 33 hours reading this book. I never regret it.
